Scam message detection

The Scam Message Detection project is designed to identify and filter out fraudulent or deceptive messages from a large set of text messages. By leveraging machine learning algorithms and natural language processing (NLP) techniques, the system can detect common patterns associated with scam messages such as phishing attempts, fraudulent offers, and suspicious links. The project utilizes a trained model to analyze incoming messages and classify them as "scam" or "safe" based on features such as keywords, URL patterns, and message context. The system can be deployed as a service on mobile or web platforms to alert users about potentially harmful messages in real-time. With the rise of online scams and phishing attacks, this project aims to reduce the risk of users falling prey to malicious messages and protect sensitive personal information. Additionally, it can be extended to include automated reporting of detected scams and integration with existing messaging platforms for proactive message screening.
Mess food wastage management

The Mess Food Waste Management project aims to reduce food wastage in messes (cafeterias) by implementing a smart system that tracks, monitors, and optimizes food usage. This system helps mess staff and management make data-driven decisions to minimize waste while ensuring efficient food preparation and distribution. The project includes features such as: Food Consumption Tracking: The system tracks the quantity of food prepared and consumed daily. It can monitor real-time data to adjust serving portions based on the actual number of students or people eating, helping to avoid over-preparation and reducing excess food waste. Waste Monitoring: By collecting data on how much food is discarded, the system can analyze patterns and identify areas where food wastage is highest. It can also suggest changes in preparation or menu to help minimize waste. Reporting and Alerts: The platform generates detailed reports on food waste trends, which can be used by mess management to make informed decisions about ingredient purchasing, portion sizes, and menu planning. Alerts can also be set for when food quantities are nearing the threshold, allowing for quick adjustments. User Feedback: The system allows users (students, staff) to provide feedback on the food quality and quantity, enabling the mess to refine its operations based on consumer preferences and waste reduction goals. By using predictive analytics and machine learning models, the system also forecasts future demand based on past data, ensuring that food preparation is optimized. This results in cost savings, reduced environmental impact, and better resource management within mess facilities.
